The concept of a pitcher win-loss record is dumb.

Let's double-down it.

The pitcher win and loss is a poorly design stat. Show why they're dumb.

But they are also historically significant. Since the beginning of baseball, one of the most repoted on stat for pitchers has been the win-loss record.

For those that don't know a winning pitcher is defined as:

The Official Scorer shall credit as the winning pitcher that pitcher whose team assumes a lead while such pitcher is in the game, or during the inning on offense in which such pitcher is removed from the game, and does not relinquish such lead...

MLB Official Rulebook, Rule 9.17(a)

The rule continues to list a few caveats and such, but I'd like to point out one thing here. There is almost no mention about pitcher performance. The only time the concept of pitcher performance is introduced is further in rule:

The Official Scorer shall not credit as the winning pitcher arelief pitcher who is ineffective in a brief appearance, when atleast one succeeding relief pitcher pitches effectively in helpinghis team maintain its lead.

MLB Official Rulebook, Rule 9.17(c)

Ok, that's just weird to me. Pitching performance matters for relievers, but not starting pitching. Why?

But even this is poorly designed. One particularly hilarious situation that by rule must result in a pitcher win is if a home pitcher blows a lead at the top or the 9th or later inning and the home team ends up walking off in the bottom half.
